Car Storage FAQ

Can I store a classic or collector car here?

Yes — our indoor drive-up bays are a popular choice for classic and collector cars. Each bay is weather-protected with LED lighting and electrical service for battery tenders and trickle chargers, and the entire property is monitored by site-wide security cameras with pin pad keyless access. It's the kind of setup you'd build at home if you had the space.

Is there electricity for a battery tender or trickle charger?

Yes. Every bay includes electrical service, so you can keep a battery tender or trickle charger running the entire time your vehicle is stored. This is especially important for classic cars, sports cars, motorcycles, and any vehicle sitting through the winter months.

How many cars fit in one bay?

Standard bays (40 ft × 14.5 ft) comfortably fit two cars parked nose-to-tail, or a single car with room for shelving, tools, or a workbench. Large bays (60 ft × 14.5 ft) fit three to four cars — ideal for small collections. For specific cars, call 412-246-4662 and we'll confirm dimensions.

Is the storage climate controlled?

Our bays are indoor and weather-protected — they shield your vehicle from UV, rain, snow, and road salt — but they are not actively climate controlled (no heat or A/C). For most cars this is ideal storage. For extremely humidity-sensitive collector vehicles, a dehumidifier plug-in is recommended and fully supported by the in-bay electrical service.

Can I access my car 24/7?

Yes. Pin pad keyless access means you can pull in and pull out any time, day or night — no scheduled gate hours, no manager to check in with. Site-wide security cameras monitor the property continuously.

Is month-to-month available, or do I need a long-term lease?

Both. Month-to-month and long-term storage are both available. Multi-season and annual discounts apply for longer commitments — call 412-246-4662 to discuss what works for your situation.

Can I store a motorcycle in a car bay?

Yes. Motorcycles, ATVs, and snowmobiles all fit easily in either bay size. If you want to store multiple motorcycles together — or combine a motorcycle with a car — a standard bay has plenty of room for both.
